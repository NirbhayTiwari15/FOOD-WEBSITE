Chole Bhature

About Food:

Chole Bhature is a popular North Indian dish consisting of spicy chickpea curry (chole) served with deep-fried
bread (bhature). The chickpeas are cooked with a blend of aromatic spices, onions, tomatoes, and
ginger-garlic paste, creating a flavorful and hearty curry. The bhature are made from flour dough, fermented
with yogurt, rolled into discs, and deep-fried until golden and puffy. This indulgent dish is often served
with pickles or onions, adding a tangy contrast to the rich curry. Chole Bhature is a beloved street food
and a favorite in Punjabi cuisine, known for its bold flavors and satisfying texture.

Ingredients:

  • Ingredients for Chole (Chickpea Curry):
  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (or 2 cans of chickpeas, drained and rinsed)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed, 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder and 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder and 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Ingredients for Bhature (Fried Bread):
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • Salt to taste and Water (as needed)
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Soak the dried chickpeas in water overnight. Rinse and drain them before cooking. If using canned chickpeas, skip this step.
  2. Pressure cook the chickpeas with enough water and a little salt until they are soft and cooked. Drain and set aside.
  3. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add the chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions are translucent.
  5. Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute and Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the mixture.
  6. Add the turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add the cooked chickpeas and mix until they are well coated with the spice mixture. Add water if needed to adjust the consistency of the curry. Simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  8. For the bhature, combine the flour, yogurt, sugar, baking powder, and salt in a bowl. Knead into a smooth dough, adding water as needed. Cover and let it rest for 2 hours.
  9. Divide the dough into small balls. Roll out each ball into a circle of about 6-7 inches in diameter.
  10. Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Fry the rolled out dough one at a time until they puff up and turn golden brown on both sides. Drain on paper towels.
  11. Serve the hot chole with the bhature. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
