Masala Dosa

About Food:

Masala Dosa is a popular South Indian dish consisting of a thin, crispy crepe made from
fermented rice and lentil batter, filled with a spiced potato mixture. It is typically served
with coconut chutney, sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew), and sometimes a spicy tomato
chutney. Masala Dosa is loved for its delicious flavor and texture, making it a favorite
breakfast or snack option in India and around the world.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/4 cup chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee for cooking
  • For the potato filling:
  • 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Curry leaves
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak the rice, urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds in water for 4-6 hours.
  2. Grind the soaked ingredients into a smooth batter. Ferment the batter overnight or for 8-10 hours.
  3. Mix the rice and urad dal batter together, add salt, and let it ferment overnight or for 8-10 hours.
  4. Heat a non-stick pan or griddle over medium heat.
  5. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the pan and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin dosa.
  6. Drizzle some oil around the edges of the dosa and cook until the bottom is golden brown.
  7. Flip the dosa and cook the other side until golden brown.
  8. Serve hot with chutney and sambar.
