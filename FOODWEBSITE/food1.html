Spaghetti Carbonara

About Food:

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish consisting of spaghetti noodles tossed in a
creamy sauce made with eggs, cheese (typically Pecorino Romano or Parmigiano-Reggiano), pancetta or
guanciale (cured pork jowl), and black pepper. The sauce is created by mixing beaten eggs with the hot pasta,
which cooks the eggs slightly, creating a rich and velvety texture. The dish is typically garnished with
additional cheese and pepper. It is known for its simple yet indulgent flavors and is a popular comfort food worldwide.

Ingredients:

  • 200g spaghetti
  • 2 eggs
  • 50g grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 50g grated Parmesan cheese
  • 100g pancetta
  • Black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti in a large pot of boiling salted water until al dente.
  2. Meanwhile, whisk the eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, Parmesan cheese, and black pepper in a bowl.
  3. In a skillet, cook the pancetta until crispy, then remove from heat.
  4. Drain the spaghetti, reserving some of the cooking water.
  5. Combine the hot spaghetti with the egg mixture, pancetta, and a splash of the cooking water. Toss until well coated and creamy.
  6. Serve immediately with additional grated cheese and black pepper.
