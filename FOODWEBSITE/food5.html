Kadhi

About Food:

Kadhi is a popular North Indian dish made with a yogurt-based gravy thickened with gram flour
(besan) and flavored with spices like fenugreek seeds, turmeric, and mustard seeds. It often
contains pakoras (gram flour fritters) and is served with steamed rice. Kadhi has a tangy and
creamy flavor, making it a comforting and satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons besan (gram flour)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 10-12 curry leaves
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Whisk together yogurt, besan, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt in a bowl to make a smooth mixture.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds, mustard seeds, fenugreek seeds, and curry leaves. Sauté for a minute.
  3. Add the chopped onion, green chilies, and ginger-garlic paste. Sauté until the onions are translucent.
  4. Add the mixed vegetables (if using) and sauté for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Pour in the yogurt mixture and stir well. Cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Adjust the consistency of the kadhi by adding water if needed. Cook for another 5-10 minutes.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice.
